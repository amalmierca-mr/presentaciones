Trend 01 — Sport as Entertainment
Trend 01 — Sport as Entertainment

Sport Is Now
An Entertainment Format

The biggest shift is not who plays sport. It is how sport is packaged, distributed and experienced. Sport no longer competes only with other sport — it competes with Netflix, Twitch, YouTube and TikTok for the same attention window.

223MSocial views on the day of WWE RAW's Netflix premiere (Netflix newsroom)
31.3MGlobal avg minute audience for Netflix NFL Christmas Day 2024
90%+Young fans use social for sport clips, highlights and live commentary (Deloitte 2025)
For Decathlon

Move from sponsoring sport to producing sport entertainment — creator-led formats, city tournaments, challenge properties and hybrid participation events designed for physical attendance and content distribution.

Kings League

Creator-led football designed for highlight culture and social conversation. Not just a competition — a fully engineered entertainment format.

La Velada del Año

Ibai turns boxing into a creator-powered event property where the bout is only one part of a much larger entertainment ecosystem.

Sidemen Charity Match / Baller League

UK and Germany show the same logic: format, talent mix and social narrative drive mass attention far beyond the sport itself.

Decathlon Opportunity

Decathlon should not only equip the game. It can create the game — owning formats that are watched, shared and revisited across markets.

Trend 02 — From Users to Tribes

The New Growth
Engine Is Belonging

People do not just want to practice. They want to join. Community has become a product, a retention model and a signal of identity. Belonging is now as important as performance — clubs, crews and shared rituals drive participation.

180M+Strava users across 185+ countries — activity as social infrastructure
10M+parkrun — an international family of over 10 million registered parkrunners
400K/wkWeekly parkrun participants across 2,600 events worldwide (2024/25 annual report)
For Decathlon

Stores can become local hubs. Categories can become clubs. Disciplines can become membership propositions. The KPI is not only footfall — it is repeat participation, belonging, advocacy and habit formation.

Adidas Runners

A global example of running as an owned community platform, not just a campaign. The product enters through the community.

Rapha Cycling Club

Membership, events, content and lifestyle all work together to create tribe value far beyond apparel.

Run clubs — Paris, Madrid, Berlin, Amsterdam, London

The local club is now both media channel and social ritual. The product enters through the community.

Decathlon Opportunity

Evolve from retailer to community orchestrator — making Decathlon the connective tissue of active life, not just the point of purchase.

Trend 03 — Sport as Everyday Lifestyle

Sport Is a
Daily Code

Sport is no longer a separate moment in the week. It is becoming a daily code: how people commute, dress, socialise, recover and signal who they are. Lifestyle brands win by being present inside routines, not waiting for campaign moments.

6h/dayMedia and entertainment time per person — sport must live inside daily routines (Deloitte 2025)
43Countries with a Decathlon second-hand offering in 2023 — circularity already part of the brand story
1.02MSecond-hand products sold by Decathlon in 2023 — proof the brand can own conscious sport
For Decathlon

Own "everyday sport": moving, commuting, outdoor living, family activity, wellness and conscious participation. That is a richer space than product category marketing — it is a platform idea that Decathlon is structurally placed to own.

On Running

A performance brand that became a lifestyle and cultural signal through design, community and premium codes.

Salomon Sportstyle

Trail heritage translated into fashion relevance and urban desirability — sport as aesthetic identity.

Decathlon Second Life

Repair, resale and buy-back reframed not as utility, but as part of a broader everyday active-life proposition — conscious participation as identity.

Decathlon Opportunity

Become the brand that makes everyday sport aspirational, accessible and shareable — across commuting, family life, circularity and urban movement.

Trend 04 — Creators as New Coaches

Creators Are
The New Leagues

The role of the athlete has not disappeared, but influence has fragmented. Discovery, explanation, motivation and fandom are increasingly mediated by creators — people who teach, test, host, challenge and entertain around disciplines.

90%+Social is already the dominant sports content touchpoint for younger fans (Deloitte 2025)
47 wksWWE RAW made the Netflix Global English TV Top 10 nearly every week in its first year
Creator-nativeThe fastest-growing sports properties are built with creators baked into the format from day one
For Decathlon

Think beyond talent casting. Build creator ecosystems by sport: coaches, challengers, testers, educators and entertainers by category and market. That creates content, commerce and credibility simultaneously.

Joe Wicks

Fitness, coaching and mass audience motivation collapsed into one creator persona — no traditional media infrastructure needed.

Pamela Reif

A global fitness creator proving that community and routine can be built across markets without legacy assets.

Ibai Ecosystem

Not influencer amplification, but the creation of new sports IPs and new audience behaviour — Kings League, La Velada del Año.

Decathlon Opportunity

Build a network of Decathlon-aligned creators by category — running, cycling, hiking, family sport — to drive discovery, trust and community at scale.

Trend 05 — Co-brands & Cultural Transfers

Sport Crosses Into
Fashion, Music & Street Culture

Some of the most relevant moves in sport today happen when sport crosses into fashion, music, design and street culture. The product becomes a cultural object. The partnership becomes the story.

PSG×JordanOne of the clearest examples of a football club becoming a global style property
Venezia×NOCTAA club explicitly positioning itself at the forefront of sport and contemporary culture
AC Milan×Off-White™PUMA framed the 2025 kit as a fusion of football, fashion and innovation
For Decathlon

Use collaborations not only to create short-term buzz, but to reframe perception. Designers, creators, athletes, cities and cultural platforms can all become partners that move the brand into more desirable, conversation-worthy spaces.

Jordan Wings × PSG

Elevated streetwear that blurs performance, fandom and luxury style codes — club as global fashion property.

Venezia FC × NOCTA

Football club, fashion language and cultural positioning working as one ecosystem. The kit is the campaign.

Decathlon × Van Rysel / KIPSTA

AG2R and UEFA already show the brand can build bridges between accessibility and high-level legitimacy — the foundation is already there.

Decathlon Opportunity

Collaborate with designers, local artists, city communities and cultural platforms to move into spaces where aspiration, identity and accessibility meet.

Trend 06 — Entertainment Giants Buy Live Sport

Live Sport Is
A Strategic Attention Product

Why are Netflix, Amazon and Apple leaning hard into sports rights? Because live sport solves the biggest problem in modern media: real-time attention, communal viewing and advertiser demand. Sport is no longer a side experiment — it is a strategic anchor.

31.3MGlobal avg minute audience for Netflix NFL Christmas Day 2024 — Ravens vs Texans
2030/31Amazon Prime Video holds UK UEFA Champions League rights through this season
Apple+MLSApple positions live F1, MLS and MLB as core Apple TV+ value — sport as ecosystem anchor
For Decathlon

Think more boldly about owned or partnerable sports moments. Not just media buying around sport, but brand properties that can be watched, shared and revisited — turning fandom into shopping, subscription and data.

Netflix

NFL Christmas Day, WWE RAW and global live-event ambition show sport is becoming a strategic attention product — not a side experiment.

Amazon Prime Video

Rights plus commerce plus ecosystem value: streaming can turn fandom into shopping, subscription and data intelligence.

Apple TV

Sport woven into a broader product ecosystem — hardware, news, music, maps, fitness and subscription value working as one platform.

Decathlon Opportunity

If media giants pay for live sport because it anchors attention, Decathlon should think about owning sport moments — not just media-buying around them.
